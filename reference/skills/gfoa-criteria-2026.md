# GFOA Distinguished Budget Presentation Award — 2026 Criteria

## Program Overview

The GFOA Distinguished Budget Presentation Award recognizes state and local governments that prepare high-quality budget documents. The 2026 program uses a points-based evaluation system with no mandatory criteria.

**Threshold:** 100+ points out of 180 total to receive the award.

---

## Content Categories (130 Points)

### 1. Community Priorities and Challenges (20 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- What are the community's most significant challenges and priorities?
- How does the budget address these challenges?
- What tradeoffs were considered in prioritizing spending?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (16-20): Clearly articulates top priorities with direct budget linkage, discusses tradeoffs
- Good (10-15): Identifies priorities but weak linkage to budget allocations
- Needs Work (0-9): Missing or generic priority statements

### 2. Value (20 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- What value does the government provide to residents?
- How is return on investment demonstrated?
- What performance measures show effectiveness?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (16-20): Clear value proposition with metrics, benchmarks, and outcome measures
- Good (10-15): Some performance data but limited context
- Needs Work (0-9): No performance measures or value statements

### 3. Long-Term Outlook (20 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- What is the long-term financial trajectory?
- How sustainable is the current budget path?
- What are the key risks and assumptions?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (16-20): 5+ year projections, fund balance analysis, risk factors, sustainability assessment
- Good (10-15): Multi-year data present but limited analysis
- Needs Work (0-9): Single-year focus only

### 4. Revenue Budget (20 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- What are the major revenue sources?
- How diversified is the revenue base?
- What assumptions drive revenue estimates?
- How do revenues compare to prior years?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (16-20): Detailed source breakdown, trend analysis, assumption documentation, rate/base analysis
- Good (10-15): Source listing with amounts but limited trend/assumption data
- Needs Work (0-9): Summary totals only

### 5. Personnel Budget (15 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- What is the total personnel cost and how is it distributed?
- How do staffing levels compare to prior years?
- What benefits and compensation changes are included?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (12-15): Position counts by department, compensation detail, FTE analysis, benefit cost breakdown
- Good (7-11): Summary personnel costs with limited detail
- Needs Work (0-6): Personnel aggregated into department totals only

### 6. Department Budget (15 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- How is the budget organized by department/function?
- What are each department's key responsibilities and goals?
- How does departmental spending align with strategic goals?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (12-15): Department narratives with goals, budget detail, and strategic alignment
- Good (7-11): Department breakdowns with some context
- Needs Work (0-6): Numbers only, no departmental context

### 7. Program Budget (15 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- What programs are funded and what do they accomplish?
- How do program goals link to organizational strategy?
- What are the program-level performance measures?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (12-15): Program descriptions with objectives, measures, and strategic linkage
- Good (7-11): Program listing with basic descriptions
- Needs Work (0-6): No program-level detail

### 8. Capital Budget (15 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- How is capital defined and applied consistently?
- What determines capital spending levels?
- How are capital needs assessed and lifecycle costs managed?
- What revenue sources fund capital projects?
- Does the budget distinguish new projects from replacements/rehabilitation?
- What details are provided for major projects (benefits, timing, funding, completion)?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (12-15): Individual project detail, funding sources, CIP schedule, lifecycle analysis
- Good (7-11): Project list with costs but limited detail
- Needs Work (0-6): Capital spending as a single line item

### 9. Budget Process (10 pts)
**Primary Questions:**
- How is the budget developed?
- What is the timeline and approval process?
- How are stakeholders engaged?

**Scoring Guidance:**
- Excellent (8-10): Process description with timeline, roles, public engagement, amendments
- Good (4-7): Basic process overview
- Needs Work (0-3): No process description

## Material Type Categories (50 Points)

### 1. Budget Document (10 pts)
- Organization and layout clarity
- Consistent formatting and design
- Effective use of headers, footers, page numbers
- Professional appearance

### 2. Budget-In-Brief/Newsletter (10 pts)
- Concise summary of key budget information
- Accessible language for general public
- Visual appeal and readability

### 3. Budget Website or Dashboard (10 pts)
- Interactive data exploration
- Responsive design
- Clear navigation
- Accessibility compliance (WCAG)

### 4. Videos (10 pts)
- Budget explanation or presentation recordings
- Accessible captioning and transcripts

### 5. Other Formats (10 pts)
- Infographics, interactive tools, community presentations
- Innovation in communication approach

---

## Key Success Factors

1. **Answer the questions** — Each content category is structured as questions stakeholders would ask. Address them directly.
2. **Show trends** — Multi-year data (3+ years) demonstrates context and trajectory.
3. **Link to strategy** — Connect budget allocations to community goals and priorities.
4. **Use visuals effectively** — Charts and graphs should clarify, not just decorate.
5. **Write for the audience** — Clear, professional prose suitable for elected officials and the public.
6. **Be comprehensive but organized** — Include detail but make it easy to find through good structure and navigation.
